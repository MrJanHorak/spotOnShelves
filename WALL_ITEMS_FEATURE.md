# Wall Items Feature - Pictures, Posters, and More!

## Overview

The Spot On Shelves app now supports hanging various types of wall items beyond just shelves, including:

- 🖼️ **Picture Frames** - Framed artwork and photos
- 📄 **Posters** - Unframed prints and posters
- ⭕ **Mirrors** - Decorative and functional mirrors
- 📺 **TVs** - Wall-mounted televisions
- 🎨 **Art Pieces** - Canvas art and other wall decor

## New Features

### 1. **Item Type Selection**

Each item can now be selected from 6 different types, with type-specific fields:

**For Shelves:**

- Width, Depth, Expected Weight
- Mounting hardware (brackets, floating, L-bracket)

**For Wall Items:**

- Width, Height, Weight
- Hanging method (wire, sawtooth, keyhole, French cleat, D-ring, bracket)
- Frame options (for pictures)

### 2. **Gallery Layout Patterns**

Choose from professional gallery layouts for your wall items:

- **Custom** - Manually position each item
- **Grid** - Evenly spaced rows and columns
- **Salon Style** - Organic, clustered arrangement with largest piece at center
- **Linear** - Single horizontal line at eye level

### 3. **Eye-Level Positioning**

Wall items are automatically positioned at optimal viewing height:

- Standard gallery height: **57 inches** to center of artwork
- Adjustable based on your preferences
- Follows professional museum standards

### 4. **Smart Hardware Recommendations**

Get intelligent hardware suggestions based on item weight:

**Lightweight (≤ 5 lbs)**

- Adhesive strips or small nails
- No wall damage for renters

**Medium (5-15 lbs)**

- Picture hanging hooks with plastic anchors
- Rated for appropriate weight

**Heavy (15-30 lbs)**

- Toggle bolts and heavy-duty anchors
- D-rings or French cleats recommended

**Very Heavy (>30 lbs)**

- **MUST mount into wall studs**
- Professional installation recommended
- French cleat or Z-bar systems

### 5. **Weight-Based Calculations**

Automatic weight estimation based on dimensions, or enter exact weight:

- Pictures: Based on size and whether framed
- Mirrors: Heavy per square inch calculation
- TVs: Estimated from screen size
- Custom weights for accuracy

### 6. **Visual Schematic Updates**

The wall schematic now shows:

- **Different colors** for each item type
- **Proper dimensions** (height and width for pictures)
- **Weight indicators** for all items
- **Bracket positions** (for shelves only)

### 7. **Hardware Installation Guide**

Detailed installation instructions including:

- Specific hardware needed
- Anchor types based on wall material
- Screw sizes
- Maximum weight capacity
- Safety warnings for heavy items

## How to Use

### Adding a Picture or Poster

1. Click **"Add Item"** button
2. Select item type (Picture, Poster, etc.)
3. Enter dimensions:
   - Width (e.g., 24 inches)
   - Height (e.g., 36 inches)
   - Weight (optional - will be estimated if blank)
4. Select hanging method (wire, D-ring, etc.)
5. For pictures: Check "Framed" if applicable

### Choosing a Gallery Layout

1. Add multiple wall items to your project
2. Scroll to the **"Gallery Layout Pattern"** section
3. Select your preferred layout:
   - **Grid**: Perfect for uniform photo collections
   - **Salon**: Best for mixed-size artwork
   - **Linear**: Great for hallways
4. Adjust **Eye Level Height** (default 57")

### Viewing Hardware Recommendations

1. After adding items, scroll to **"Hardware Recommendations"**
2. Review specific hardware for each item:
   - Hardware type (hooks, cleats, etc.)
   - Anchor recommendations
   - Screw sizes
   - Safety notes
3. See max weight capacity for each installation method

## Best Practices

### For Pictures & Art

- **Standard height**: 57-60 inches to center of artwork
- **Gallery walls**: Start with largest piece, work outward
- **Spacing**: 2-6 inches between pieces for salon style
- **Wire hanging**: Keep wire taut for stability

### For Mirrors

- **Heavy items**: Always use appropriate anchors or studs
- **Safety**: Consider adding safety backing film for large mirrors
- **Position**: Eye level varies - consider who uses it most

### For TVs

- **ALWAYS mount into studs** - no exceptions
- Use TV-specific wall mounts rated for your TV size/weight
- Consider viewing angle and seating position
- Cable management: plan before mounting

### Mixed Installations (Shelves + Pictures)

- Place shelves first, then position pictures around them
- Ensure shelves are at appropriate viewing/reach height
- Pictures can go above or below shelves
- Maintain visual balance across the wall

## Safety Warnings

⚠️ **Heavy Items (>30 lbs)**

- MUST mount into wall studs
- Use stud finder before installation
- Consider professional installation
- Do not rely on drywall anchors alone

⚠️ **Wall Material Matters**

- Drywall: Use appropriate anchors, or mount to studs
- Plaster: Use molly bolts or toggle anchors
- Concrete/Brick: Requires masonry anchors and hammer drill
- Always verify wall type before drilling

⚠️ **Earthquake/Child Safety**

- Secure heavy items with earthquake-resistant hardware
- Consider safety straps for TVs
- Keep heavy items away from play areas
- Regular inspection of mounting hardware

## Tips & Tricks

1. **Paper Templates**: Cut paper to item size and tape to wall to visualize before drilling
2. **Level Everything**: A small bubble level is essential
3. **Mark Lightly**: Use pencil, then darken when confirmed
4. **Stud Finder**: Invest in a good electronic stud finder
5. **Drill Bits**: Use correct size for your anchors
6. **Helper**: Have someone help with large/heavy items

## Technical Notes

### Supported Hanging Methods

- **Wire**: Traditional picture wire with hooks
- **Sawtooth**: Built-in metal hanger on back
- **Keyhole**: Slot-style hanger
- **French Cleat**: Interlocking wooden strips
- **D-Ring**: Metal rings on back corners
- **Bracket**: Direct bracket mounting

### Gallery Layouts Explained

**Grid Layout**

- Items arranged in even rows/columns
- Consistent spacing throughout
- Best for uniform collections

**Salon Layout**

- Largest piece at center (eye level)
- Smaller pieces clustered around
- Organic, artistic arrangement
- Common in art galleries

**Linear Layout**

- All items aligned at same height
- Centers at eye level (57")
- Perfect for hallways or above furniture

**Custom Layout**

- Manual positioning
- Full control over placement
- User-defined based on preferences
